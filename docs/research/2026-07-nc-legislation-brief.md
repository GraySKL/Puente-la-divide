## The three laws (all enacted over governors' vetoes)

### 1. HB 10 — SL 2024-55 (effective Dec 1, 2024) — high confidence
All 100 NC sheriffs must (a) **honor ICE detainers** — holding people up to 48 hours
for federal pickup — and (b) **notify ICE** when they cannot determine the immigration
status of someone *charged* (not convicted) with certain felonies, 50B
domestic-violence protective-order violations, and specific A1 misdemeanors.
Cooper veto overridden Nov 19–20, 2024 (House 72-44, Senate 30-19). As of Feb 2026 all
counties report compliance (NC Sheriffs' Association).
Sources: https://www.ncleg.gov/EnactedLegislation/SessionLaws/HTML/2023-2024/SL2024-55.html ·
https://www.ncleg.gov/BillLookUp/2023/H10 ·
https://www.acluofnorthcarolina.org/legislation/hb-10-require-sheriffs-cooperate-ice/ ·
WUNC (Nov 2024): https://www.wunc.org/race-class-communities/2024-11-25/immigration-enforcement-house-bill-10-north-carolina

### 2. HB 318 — SL 2025-85, "Criminal Illegal Alien Enforcement Act" (effective Oct 1, 2025) — high confidence
Broadens the jail-intake status-check trigger to **any felony charge plus any
impaired-driving charge**; extends the detainer hold to **48 hours past the scheduled
release time** with a 2-hour advance ICE notification; grants NC officers/agencies
**immunity from civil or criminal liability** for detainer-related actions.
Stein veto overridden Jul 29, 2025 (House 72-48, Senate 30-19).
**Do not cite an initial floor-passage vote tally — the one in circulation failed
verification (1-2 refuted); only the override tallies are confirmed.**
Sources: https://www.ncleg.gov/EnactedLegislation/SessionLaws/PDF/2025-2026/SL2025-85.pdf ·
https://www.ncleg.gov/BillLookUp/2025/H318 · WUNC Oct 2025 + Daily Tar Heel Aug 2025 coverage.

### 3. SB 153 — SL 2026-19, "North Carolina Border Protection Act" (LAW since Jun 24, 2026) — high confidence
- **First NC law to mandate formal 287(g) agreements** — for four STATE agencies:
  Department of Public Safety, Department of Adult Correction, **State Highway
  Patrol**, and SBI (NOT county sheriffs). Each must also run a status-check
  pipeline: attempt to determine status of people in custody/under supervision,
  query ICE when unable, and hand over requested information.
- Separately: a county or city keeping a prohibited "sanctuary" ordinance **waives
  its governmental immunity** from tort suits if an "unauthorized alien" commits a
  crime there (new G.S. 153A-435.1 / 160A-485.1) — even without insurance.
- **Unusual dating nuance:** Senate override Jul 29, 2025; House completed the
  override 11 months later, Jun 24, 2026 (71-47) — that is when it became law.
  Any source from between those dates calling SB 153 "pending" or "not law" is
  stale, not wrong.
Sources: https://www.ncleg.gov/BillLookup/2025/S153 ·
https://www.ncleg.gov/Sessions/2025/Bills/Senate/PDF/S153v4.pdf ·
https://www.acluofnorthcarolina.org/press-releases/ncga-overrides-governors-veto-on-sb-153-expands-ice-powers-despite-documented-abuses-and-no-accountability/

## What this means day-to-day (Track B + verified operational reality)

- **Traffic stops carry more weight now.** The State Highway Patrol is mandated into
  a 287(g) agreement (SB 153), and Siembra NC's toolkit reports ICE has "escalated
  their tactics in making community arrests, especially targeting people while
  driving in their cars." The app's La parada guidance (silence, agency question,
  calm body) is the response — this section explains *why it matters extra in NC*.
  Source: https://www.siembranc.org/en-toolkit/defend-yourself-from-ice-and-know-your-rights
- **Any felony or DUI charge → status check + possible 48-hour hold** past scheduled
  release in every NC county jail (HB 10 + HB 318). Being *charged* is enough.
- **"My county doesn't have 287(g)" no longer means much**: HB10/HB318 cooperation is
  mandatory statewide regardless of 287(g) status (ACLU-NC 287(g) Toolkit, Mar 2026:
  https://www.acluofnorthcarolina.org/app/uploads/2026/03/Final-3.12.26-287g-Toolkit.pdf).
  287(g) signings are also accelerating (~23 active as of Sept 2025 per Enlace Latino
  NC — **any specific count is time-sensitive and disputed; re-verify before citing**).
- **ICE arrests in NC reportedly doubled in 2025**, largely funneled through local
  jails under the new cooperation laws (WUNC Dec 2025 / Enlace Latino NC).
- **A federal class action is fighting back:** Aceituno v. USDHS (filed Feb 24, 2026,
  W.D.N.C., case 3:26-cv-00146; ACLU-NC, ACLU, Democracy Forward, Southern Coalition
  for Social Justice) challenges warrantless ICE/CBP arrests across NC tied to the
  Nov 2025 Charlotte enforcement surge. It challenges federal practices, not these
  state laws. https://www.aclu.org/cases/aceituno-v-usdhs
- **Advocacy framing, label as such if used:** ACLU-NC's position that 287(g)-type
  agreements "have contributed to documented rights violations" (citing the 2012
  DOJ Alamance County findings) is org framing (2-1 vote), not a court holding.
