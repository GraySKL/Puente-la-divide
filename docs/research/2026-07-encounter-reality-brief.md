# Encounter-reality research brief — July 2026

Sourcing basis for deepening Puente la Divide's scenarios beyond generic KYR scripts.
Produced 2026-07-09 by a multi-agent research pass (109 agents; every claim below
survived 3-voter adversarial verification unless noted). **Track A** = documented
reality (journalism/oversight). **Track B** = actionable guidance (rights orgs only).
App content rule: *what happens* may cite Track A; *what to do* must cite Track B.

## How to read confidence

- **high** = 3-0 verification votes, independently cross-corroborated.
- **medium** = 2-1 votes or existence-only verification — cite that the resource
  exists; pull the underlying document before extracting tactical detail.
- **REFUTED** = failed verification. Listed so nobody re-adds them by accident.

## 1) Vehicle stops — *La parada*

**Track A (what happens) — high confidence**
- ProPublica documented **~50 instances of agents breaking vehicle windows in the
  first ~6 months of 2025**, vs. 8 in the entire prior decade with identical methods.
  Includes a named ICE official (Matthew Elliston) telling a TV reporter during a
  Maryland stop "we'll smash the … window out and drag him out" before doing so
  (video-documented). Source (verifier-corrected URL):
  https://projects.propublica.org/trump-ice-smashed-windows-deportation-arrests/
  Cross-corroborated: Reason, AP/Yahoo, Baltimore Banner/CBS Baltimore.
  ProPublica caveats the count is not comprehensive (no government stats exist).
- **REFUTED — do not use:** "at least 10 people injured in these incidents" (1-2 vote).
  No verified injury count exists. Cite only the incident-count comparison.
- **NOT VERIFIED in this pass:** shooting deaths during 2025–2026 stops (e.g. the
  reported Chicago-area and Minneapolis cases). No claim survived verification here —
  needs a dedicated, narrowly-scoped follow-up before any in-app reference.

**Track B (what to do) — high confidence**
- ILRC: remain calm, **do not run** — fleeing can be used against you. Separately and
  explicitly **assert the right to remain silent out loud**; decline questions about
  birthplace/status until speaking with a lawyer.
  https://www.ilrc.org/community-resources/know-your-rights/know-your-rights-when-confronted-ice-flyer
  https://www.ilrc.org/sites/default/files/2024-11/Step-by-Step%20Family%20Preparedness%20Plan_ENG_FULL_Nov%202024.pdf
- NILC (May 2025): immigration enforcement is now also carried out by **non-ICE
  agencies** (local police, DEA), and agents **commonly identify themselves only as
  "police"** — explicitly ask *which agency* the officer represents.
  https://media.nilc.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/KYR-What-To-Do-if-Immigration-Approaches-Arrests-or-Detains-You-ENG-May-2025.pdf

## 2) Home / the door — *La casa*

**Track A (what happens)**
- Agents misrepresent themselves as "police" (see NILC above; corroborated by ACLU-SoCal's
  dedicated ICE-impersonation page).
- **Live legal dispute — flag in-app as contested, not settled:** a reported 2025–2026
  DHS memo asserts administrative warrants (I-205) authorize forced home entry against
  people with final removal orders. Referenced in CNN/NBC/ABC/Protect Democracy
  reporting per verifier notes; the memo claim itself was not independently verified
  in this pass. Rights-org guidance below describes the settled doctrine
  (Payton v. New York, 1980) that this memo tries to depart from.

**Track B (what to do) — high confidence, unanimous across orgs**
- **Administrative warrants (DHS Form I-200/I-205, signed by an ICE officer or
  immigration judge) do NOT authorize home entry without consent. Only a judicial
  warrant — signed by a judge, naming the correct person and address — does.**
  Stated identically by AIC, NILC, ILRC, NIJC, ACLU-NC (6 of 7 sub-claims 3-0).
- **Do not open the door, even partially.** Talk through it; ask for any claimed
  warrant to be slid under the door or shown through a window/peephole. NIJC
  explicitly extends the protocol to children.
  https://immigrantjustice.org/for-immigrants/know-your-rights/ice-encounter/
- ACLU-NC hosts annotated example documents (Jun 3, 2025) showing a valid judicial
  warrant vs. ICE Form I-200 marked "NOT VALID… not signed by a judge" — verified by
  downloading the PDF. https://www.acluofnorthcarolina.org/know-your-rights/kyr-ir/

## 3) Workplace raids — *El trabajo*

**Track A (what happens) — high confidence**
- 2025 worksite enforcement surged: **40+ actions, 1,100+ arrests in 7 months**,
  culminating in the Sept 4, 2025 Ellabell, GA Hyundai battery-plant raid
  (~475 arrested — DHS's largest single-site action ever).
  https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Worksite-Enforcement-Factsheet-2025.pdf
  Corroborated: NBC, CNN, immpolicytracking.org.

**Track B (what to do)**
- The judicial/administrative warrant distinction applies to the **non-public areas of
  a workplace** (same sources as §2). A May 2025 federal district-court ruling rejected
  a "Blackie's warrant" worksite-entry attempt.
- **Open question (needs follow-up before deep scenario):** org guidance on conduct
  *during* an active raid — collateral-arrest risk for bystander workers, movement/exit
  rights, whether to state you are not the named target.

## Cross-cutting: family preparedness — high confidence

ILRC + United We Dream converge (all sub-claims 3-0):
- Share your **A-Number** with trusted contacts in advance; know ICE's online detainee locator.
- Keep a completed **preparedness packet** in a locked place ICE cannot access.
- Carry valid work-permit/status proof; **never foreign or false documents**.
- Designate an **emergency contact** with passcodes and house keys.
  https://www.ilrc.org/community-resources/know-your-rights/step-step-family-preparedness-plan
  https://unitedwedream.org/resources/stay-ready-with-a-preparedness-packet/

Also: Siembra NC hosts an ICE-encounter video resource (existence verified, content not
extracted) — good for crediting/cross-linking. https://siembranc.org/

**REFUTED — re-verify before use:** the specific formulation "ILRC advises carrying a
red card while carrying no documents about country of origin" (1-2). Red cards are
well-known KYR tools, but re-source directly from https://www.ilrc.org/redcards before
putting red-card guidance in scenario text.

## Tone: how orgs communicate life-threatening risk without terror

The clearest verified pattern (ILRC): **separate physical compliance from rights
assertion.** Body calm — don't run, don't resist, hands visible. Voice firm — say the
rights out loud ("I want to remain silent"), keep the door closed, ask for the warrant.
The danger is named plainly (windows do get broken; agents do claim to be police) but
every warning is paired immediately with a concrete, practicable action. No sirens,
no catastrophizing — preparation as an act of care for one's family.
