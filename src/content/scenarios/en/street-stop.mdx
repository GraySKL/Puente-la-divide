## The situation

You are walking, on public transit, or in a store parking lot. An officer stops you and starts asking questions.

## Your rights

- You have the **right to remain silent**. You do not have to answer questions about where you were born, your immigration status, or how you entered the country.
- You have the right to **ask if you are free to leave**. If yes, walk away calmly.
- You do **not** have to consent to a search of your body, bag, or belongings.
- Do **not** run, resist, or obstruct, even if you believe the stop is unfair.
- Do **not** lie or show false documents.

## What to say

- "Am I free to leave?"
- "I want to remain silent."
- "I do not consent to a search of my belongings."
- "I want to speak with a lawyer."
- "What is your name and badge number?"

## After

- Write down or voice-record what happened as soon as it is safe.
- Note the officer's name, badge number, agency, time, and location.
- Contact a trusted org and a lawyer if you were detained, searched, or asked about status.
