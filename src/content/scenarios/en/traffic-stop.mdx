## The situation

You are driving and an officer pulls you over. They may be local police, state police, or ICE.

## Your rights

- The driver must provide a **driver's license, registration, and proof of insurance**. Passengers do **not** have to identify themselves unless state law requires it (laws vary — when in doubt, ask "Am I free to leave?").
- You have the **right to remain silent** about your immigration status.
- You do **not** have to consent to a search of your car. They need probable cause or your consent.
- Do **not** run or resist — even if the stop is unfair.

## What to say

- "I want to remain silent."
- "I do not consent to a search of my car."
- "Am I free to leave?"
- "I want to speak with a lawyer."

## How to behave

- Pull over safely. Turn off the engine. Turn on the interior light at night.
- Keep your hands visible on the steering wheel.
- Tell the officer before you reach for documents.
- Do not open the glove box or center console until they ask.

## After

- Write down the officer's name, badge number, agency, and what was said.
- If you were searched without consent, contact a lawyer.
