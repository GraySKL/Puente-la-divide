## The situation

ICE arrives at your job. They may be talking to your supervisor, walking through public areas, or asking workers questions.

## Your rights

- ICE may enter **public areas** of a business without a warrant. They **cannot enter private areas** (back rooms, employee-only spaces) without either a **judicial warrant** signed by a judge or the employer's permission.
- You have the **right to remain silent** about your immigration status, where you were born, and how you entered the country.
- You do **not** have to give ICE your real name if you do not want to — but do **not** lie or show false documents. The safest move is silence and "I want a lawyer."
- You do not have to consent to a search of your bag or belongings.

## What to say

- "I want to remain silent."
- "I want to speak with a lawyer."
- "I do not consent to a search of my belongings."
- "Am I free to leave?"

## After

- Write down what happened: who came, what they did, who they spoke to, who was detained.
- Notify your trusted org. Tell co-workers about their rights.
- If ICE entered a private area without a judicial warrant, tell a lawyer — that may be unlawful.
