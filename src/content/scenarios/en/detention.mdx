## The situation

You have been detained by ICE or transferred into immigration custody.

## Your rights

- You have the right to **remain silent**. Use it.
- You have the right to a **lawyer** — but the government does **not** provide one in immigration court. You have the right to call one and to be represented at your own expense.
- You have the right to **make a phone call**, including to a lawyer or your country's consulate.
- You do **not** have to **sign anything** — especially a "voluntary departure" or "stipulated removal" — without a lawyer reading it first. Signing can give up your right to see a judge.

## What to say

- "I want to remain silent."
- "I want to speak with a lawyer."
- "I want to make a phone call."
- "I want to contact my country's consulate."
- "I will not sign anything without speaking to a lawyer first."
- "Please leave the document you want me to sign and I will look at it with my lawyer."

## What to memorize before this happens

- The phone number of a lawyer or family member. Your phone may be taken from you.
- Your A-number (Alien Registration Number) if you have one.
- The name of a trusted local org (e.g., Siembra NC).

## After / for family on the outside

- Use the [ICE Online Detainee Locator](https://locator.ice.gov) to find a detained person — search by A-number or name + country of birth.
- Contact a trusted immigration legal services org as quickly as possible.
- Do **not** post on social media in ways that share the detained person's status, location, or details without their family's consent.
