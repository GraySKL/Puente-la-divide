## The situation

Someone knocks loudly. They say they are ICE or "police." They want you to open the door.

## Your rights

- **You do not have to open the door** unless ICE has a **judicial warrant signed by a judge** with your name and address on it.
- An **administrative ICE warrant** (Form I-200 or I-205) is **not** a judicial warrant. ICE cannot enter your home with one.
- You can ask them to slide the warrant under the door so you can read it.
- You have the right to remain silent. You do not have to answer questions about where you were born or your immigration status.

## What to say (do not open the door)

> Speak loudly through the closed door.

- "Please slide the warrant under the door so I can read it."
- "Is that a judicial warrant signed by a judge, or an administrative warrant?"
- "I do not give you permission to enter my home."
- "I do not wish to speak with you."
- "I want to speak with a lawyer."

## After

- Memorize a lawyer or family member's phone number — your phone can be taken.
- Write down badge numbers, names, and what was said as soon as it is safe to do so.
- Contact a trusted local org (e.g., Siembra NC, your nearest immigrants' rights group) and a lawyer.
